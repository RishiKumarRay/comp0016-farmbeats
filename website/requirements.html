Requirements

Project Background

Avanade is a company that provides IT services and innovative solutions based on technologies such as the Cloud and AI analytics on Microsoft platforms. Our project is titled IoT Farmbeats, which focuses on finding solutions to improve current farming processes by digitalising agriculture.

Why are IoT Farms important?

With the world population increasing annually, it is essential to increase food production to keep everyone well fed. It is predicted that the world population may raise up to 9.7 billion by 2050, allowing the demand for food production to increase by 69%. Hence, it is evident that farm processes must be optimised. We can significantly increase the efficiency of farm processes by introducing the concept of smart farming. This means using many IoT sensors to precisely monitor conditions needed to grow plants or to take care of livestock.

Client requirements

Our team arranged weekly meetings with Avanade to discuss their requirements. We completed mindmaps and concept boards to explore different aspects of farming, such as livestock or agriculture. We thought about possible solutions to improve these by matching these with a user, such as a farmer or a researcher.

We asked questions such as:

Who will our users be?
What area in farming should we focus on?
What aspects of technology can we use?
How can our solution optimise farming?

We were handed an AI camera and a Microsoft raspberry pi toolkit to explore the technology we can use for our solution. After a few meetings discussing different potential ideas, we decided to focus on creating a control system for optimal plant growth with many sensors monitoring the condition, using an AI camera to monitor the health of the plant.

Project Goal

Our aim is to research ways to optimise plant growth and maintain plant health in a closed environment through smart farming techniques. This includes a system that automatically maintains factors such as temperature and humidity based on optimal values inputted by the user. There will also be a user interface that allows users to view sensor values and data analytics, demonstrating the environmental conditions and the health state of each plant. We are starting our project with a small scale experiment, but intend to deploy our solution in larger farm industries.
